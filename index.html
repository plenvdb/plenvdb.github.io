PlenVDB

A Memory Efficient VDB-Based Radiance Fields for Fast Training and Rendering

CVPR 2023

  • Han Yan
  • Celong Liu
  • Chao Ma
  • Xing Mei

  • Paper

  • Code

In this paper, we present a new neural radiance field representation that aims to accelerate both the training and the inference processes with VDB, a well-established hierarchical data structure for sparse volumes. VDB is designed to enjoy the best of both sparse and dense volumes: compact data representation, and efficient random and spatially coherent data access, making it a promising data structure for NeRF data interpolation and ray casting. Our method, Plenoptic VDB (PlenVDB), directly learns the VDB data structure from a set of images with known poses with a novel training strategy and uses it for real-time rendering. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and the efficiency of our method over previous methods: first, it converges faster in the training process; second, it delivers a more compact data format for NeRF data presentation; finally, it renders more efficiently on commodity graphics hardware. Our mobile PlenVDB demo achieves 30+ fps, 1280x720 px resolution on an iPhone12 mobile phone.

Given a set of training views, our method directly optimizes a VDB model. Then a novel view can be rendered with the model. The potential of VDB is due to two advantages: fast voxel access for faster speed, and efficient storage for smaller model size. This enables efficient NeRF rendering on mobile devices.
